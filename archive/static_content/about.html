What is The UK Meteor Data Archive

The archive collects data from cameras in the UK Meteor Network and other UK and Western European locations, and processes it to determine shower associations, orbits and trajectories. From this we can analyse meteor showers, search for new showers or outbursts, and track fireballs. We can also (sometimes) help find meteorites!

Capturing meteors

As meteors streak across the sky contributors' cameras detect their motion and record video clips capturing these spectacular event. The video clips are then analysed and from these we can determine basic information about the meteor. The magic happens when a meteor is recorded from different locations; by triangulation we can calculate altitudes, velocities, and even the orbit of the meteoroid around the sun before it encountered our atmosphere.

Teamwork

We collaborate with other other groups around the world, notably the Global Meteor Network, but also networks in Europe, North America, Australia, New Zealand and Asia. When an interesting event happens anywhere in the world, our team are ready to help collect data, analyse it and hopefully help the local group find meteorites on the ground.

>p>Closer to home, we have partnered with the Natural History Museum.

Why we do it?

There are lots of reasons but primarily we are all passionate about astronomy and the night sky. Our data is making a valuable contribution to the understanding of meteors and the solar system and in this respect we are all Citizen Scientists.

But let's not also forget that it's FUN too! Checking what your camera captured overnight is the astronomy equivalent to fishing and there is real excitement when you discover that you captured a bright fireball.
